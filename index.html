The Impact of:
Social Media on
Mental Health

Group 4

Thesis Statement

Social media has a significant impact on mental health, offering both positive and negative effects. While it helps people connect, share, and express themselves, it can also contribute to stress, comparison, and self-esteem issues.

Social media has changed how people connect and perceive themselves. It provides new ways to communicate, build communities, and find inspiration. However, it can also lead to emotional pressure and unrealistic expectations. Understanding both the positive and negative effects helps us use social media more wisely.

Positive Impacts:

Connection and support: Social media allows people to stay in touch with friends and family, no matter the distance.

Education and awareness: It helps spread awareness about mental health and encourages young people to seek help.

Creativity and motivation: Platforms like TikTok and Instagram allow users to share their talents, ideas, and stories.

Negative Impacts:

Anxiety and depression: Excessive social media use is linked to higher levels of anxiety, stress, and depression.

Cyberbullying: Online harassment causes emotional distress and fear among users.

Addiction and poor focus: Social media addiction reduces focus, affects sleep, and increases fatigue.

In conclusion, social media plays a double role in mental health — it can empower and connect people, but it can also create stress and emotional problems. The key is moderation: balancing online activities with real-life interactions helps maintain a healthy mind.
